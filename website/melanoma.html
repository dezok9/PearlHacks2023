MELANOMA

(n.) cancer of the skin believed to be caused by overexposure to harmful UV rays and, in part, by genetic factors

Historically, melanoma has only been shown on lighter skin tones in textbooks, medical documentation, and on online resources. Without doubt, this has made diagnosing melanoma on darker skin tones much more difficult and given skin cancer a higher mortality rate for those with darker skin tones.

     Although Black individuals and people with darker skin tones are statistically less likely to develop melanoma, African Americans are nearly 3x more likely to die of this aggressive form of cancer than their Hispanic or White counterparts.

     The reason? Melanoma is frequently missed on darker skin. Look to the interactive model to the right. Could you identify the melanoma patch on the hand (if not, look a bit closer at the ring finger)? Many practitioners often cannot because of faults in medical documentation on melanoma on darker skin tones, leading to missed cases of melanoma and late treatment of this type of cancer.
